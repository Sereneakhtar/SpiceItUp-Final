Helpful Cooking Tips

Tip 1: Salt As You Go

  • Seasoning your food with salt as you cook can help you taste and adjust the flavor at each stage.
  • Salting meat 30 minutes before cooking can help retain the moisture. FOr vegetabbles like zucchine or eggplant, it gets the excess water of them so they brown without getting soggy.
  • This allows for more control over the final taste and prevents the need to add excessive salt at the end.
  • If you accidentally oversalt the dish, don't worry! You can balance it by adding a little bit of lemon juice or vinegar. Or you can dilute the dish with some water. Potatoes tend to help soak out the salt if theres too much.

Tip 2: Prep Before Starting

  • Cooking becomes a reward, not a chore, when you have everything prepare ahead of time.
  • Making sure you have all your ingredients measured and ready to go before you start cooking can make the process smoother and more enjoyable.
  • It allows you to focus on the cooking itself rather than scrambling to find ingredients or measure them while you're in the middle of a recipe.
  • This can lead to better results and a more enjoyable cooking experience.
  • This is vital for stir-fries where things move fast!

Tip 3: Dry Ingredients vs. Wet Ingredients

  • Understanding the difference between dry and wet ingredients can help you measure them more accurately.
  • Dry ingredients like flour, sugar, and salt are typically measured by cups, teaspoons, or tablespoons. For best accuracy, scoop dry ingredient till it overflows, then use a flat edge to scrape off the excess.
  • Wet ingredients like milk, oil, and water are measured in liquid measuring cups. Make sure the cup is on a flat surface and your eyes are level with the fill line.
